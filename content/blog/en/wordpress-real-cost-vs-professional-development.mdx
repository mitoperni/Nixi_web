Thinking about building your website with WordPress because "it's free"? It's a very common decision, and it makes sense at first glance. But there's a catch: WordPress itself is free, but **a professional WordPress website is not**. Let's break down the real numbers so you can make an informed decision.

## The "free WordPress" myth

When someone says WordPress is free, they're talking about the software itself. But to have a working website, you need much more than that.

### What you absolutely need

**Hosting:** Cheap shared hosting costs between €80 and €150 per year. But if you want your site to load fast and not go down when you get traffic, you need at least managed WordPress hosting, which runs around €200-€400 per year (WP Engine, Kinsta, SiteGround…).

**Domain:** Between €10 and €20 per year. This one is cheap.

**Premium theme:** Most free themes are functional but limited. A quality premium theme costs between €50 and €100 (one-time). But be careful: Elementor or Divi themes have annual subscriptions of €50-€200.

**Essential plugins:**
- SEO plugin (Yoast, RankMath): €79-€129/year for Pro version
- Security plugin (Wordfence, iThemes): €99-€199/year
- Backup plugin (UpdraftPlus, BackupBuddy): €70-€99/year
- Cache plugin (WP Rocket): €59/year
- Form plugin (Gravity Forms): €59/year
- Anti-spam: €10-€40/year

Just with these basic plugins you're already at **€376-€626 per year** just in licenses.

### The cost of maintenance

Here's the part nobody mentions. WordPress requires constant updates: the WordPress core, themes, and plugins. Each update can break something on your website (and it happens more than you'd imagine).

If you hire a basic maintenance service (updates, backups, monitoring), the price in Spain in 2025 is around **€30-€100 per month**, that is, between €360 and €1,200 per year.

Do you do it yourself? You're investing time —your time, which has value— in technical tasks instead of in your business.

### Summary of real annual WordPress costs

| Item | Estimated annual cost |
|---|---|
| Managed hosting | €200 – €400 |
| Domain | €12 – €20 |
| Essential plugins | €376 – €626 |
| Premium theme | €50 – €200 |
| Technical maintenance | €360 – €1,200 |
| **Total** | **€998 – €2,446/year** |

And that's the best case, not counting unexpected costs: the hacked website that needs cleaning (between €150 and €500), the update that breaks the site and requires hours of work, or the plugin that loses support and needs replacement.

## What about a professionally developed website?

With a custom-built website using Next.js, annual maintenance costs are significantly lower:

- Hosting on Vercel or similar: **€0-€20/month** (many projects run on the free plan)
- No plugins to update
- No annual plugin licenses
- Minimal technical maintenance because there are no third-party dependencies

The upfront cost is higher (the initial development investment), but the **total cost over 3-5 years is comparable or lower**, with a faster, more secure website without WordPress limitations.

## Is WordPress worth it then?

WordPress has its place. It's a good option for personal blogs, budget projects and people with time and motivation to learn. But for a serious business that needs a professional web presence, the real costs add up quickly.
