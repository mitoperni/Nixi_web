"I'll build the website myself, WordPress is very easy." We've heard this many times. And we won't lie to you: for certain cases, it makes sense. But for a business that wants to use its website as a real customer acquisition tool, the difference between building it yourself and hiring a professional is enormous.

## What you get when you build it yourself with WordPress

When you set up your website with WordPress (or Wix, or Squarespace), you get:

- A website with a template appearance (even if you customise it, it shows)
- Mediocre performance on mobile devices
- Basic SEO, but without the technical structure Google rewards
- Dependency on plugins for anything
- You yourself as responsible for updates, security and maintenance
- Nobody to call when something breaks

The time you invest in learning to use the tool, configuring plugins, solving problems and keeping the site updated is time you're not dedicating to your business.

## What working with a professional developer includes

### 1. Analysis and strategy before writing a line of code

A good developer starts by understanding your business: who your customers are, what they search for on Google, what the competition does and what you want to achieve with your website.

This analysis determines the structure of the site, the content of each section and how it should be optimised to rank. It's not just "making a pretty website": it's building a customer acquisition tool.

### 2. Technical SEO from the start

Technical SEO isn't just keywords. It's:

- Correct and semantic URL structure
- Optimised metadata for each page
- Structured data (schema markup) so Google understands the content
- Optimal loading speed (Core Web Vitals)
- Sitemap and robots.txt correctly configured
- HTTPS and security certificates
- Perfect mobile version (mobile-first indexing)

All of this from day one. Not as a layer added afterwards.

### 3. Design adapted to your brand, not a template

Your website has to communicate who you are and why the customer should choose you. A custom design adapts colours, typography, visual structure and messages to your brand and target audience.

A WordPress template communicates that you use WordPress. And your competitors know it too.

### 4. Professional performance

As we've seen in previous articles, a custom website with Next.js can reach 95-100/100 on PageSpeed. This translates directly into better Google rankings and more conversions.

### 5. Support and maintenance

When something fails, you have someone to call. Not a WordPress support forum, not a ticket to a hosting service. A person who knows your website, who designed it and who can solve the problem.

### 6. Website evolution with your business

Your business changes. You launch new services, change focus, need new functionality. With a professional developer, your website evolves with you without having to rebuild it from scratch.

## The real investment: comparing over 3 years

**Self-managed WordPress:**

- Initial setup time: 40-80 hours of your work

- Result: a mediocre-performance website that you maintain

Building your website yourself isn't free: it costs time, money in tools and missed opportunities from a mediocre web presence.
